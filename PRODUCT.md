# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers and hiring managers viewing this as a portfolio project, alongside general Sudoku players who use the app directly. The developer audience evaluates it as evidence of full-stack competence and design sensibility. The player audience is casual — they want to pick up and solve a puzzle without friction.

## Product Purpose

An interactive Sudoku web app that generates uniquely-solvable puzzles across three difficulty levels, provides hint-by-hint assistance, solves puzzles on demand, and supports a custom puzzle creation and validation mode. Success looks like: a player can flow from new game to solved without confusion, and a portfolio reviewer sees clearly intentional, polished craft.

## Brand Personality

Confident, Precise, Refined.

The tone is that of a skilled craftsperson — restrained, not decorative. No showboating, but the quality of every detail signals competence. The design should make an experienced engineer nod rather than a casual user gasp.

## Anti-references

- **Overcrowded game UIs**: no ads, badges, streak counters, pop-ups, or dark patterns. The mobile-game aesthetic is the opposite of what this should feel like.
- **Generic template aesthetics**: default rounded-card layouts, safe palettes, and gradient buttons that read as "used a UI kit."
- **Skeuomorphic puzzle aesthetics**: no paper textures, pencil-line graphics, or literal newspaper pastiche. The grid itself is physical enough.

## Design Principles

1. **The grid is the hero.** Every design decision defers to the puzzle board. Chrome should disappear when the user is playing.
2. **States are first-class citizens.** Selected, given, user-filled, error, hint, and solved are distinct and deliberate — not afterthoughts.
3. **Restraint as craft.** What's removed shows as much skill as what's added. Resist decoration.
4. **Keyboard and touch parity.** The experience must feel equally complete on a laptop with no mouse and on a phone.
5. **Portfolio-grade polish.** The details — focus rings, transition timing, disabled states, empty states — are the signal. They're not optional.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text at 4.5:1 minimum against background. Large text and UI controls at 3:1. Visible focus indicators on all interactive elements. Keyboard navigation for all game actions. Color must not be the sole error indicator — structural distinction required alongside color change.
